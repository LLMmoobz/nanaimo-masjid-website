About the Centre

Serving the Muslim Community Across Mid-Vancouver Island

The Islamic Centre of Nanaimo serves the Mid-Vancouver Island region, from Duncan in the south to Tofino in the west and Port Hardy in the north.

Our Mission

A Masjid Rooted in Worship and Service

Spiritual Life

To provide a welcoming place for daily prayers, Friday prayer, Ramadan programs, and Eid gatherings.

Education

To support Quran learning, halaqahs, youth development, and classes for the community.

Community Support

To promote the values of peace, compassion, and unity while serving families, students, and the wider community.

Our History

How the Nanaimo Muslim Community Grew

1970s

Early Muslim Presence

Muslims have been living in Nanaimo since at least 1970. Over time, families and individuals built the relationships that later formed an organized Muslim community.

1989

Organized Community Life Begins

The first organized Muslim community in Nanaimo was established in 1989 by two dedicated individuals. At that time, the community consisted of only a few families who began by celebrating important Islamic occasions. Soon after, regular Friday prayers were held on a rotating basis in family homes.

1997

Growth and Organization

In 1997, the Islamic community of Nanaimo joined the Vancouver Island Muslim Association in Victoria. Two years later, in 1999, that association became a chapter of the B.C. Muslim Association.

2000

The First Islamic Centre Opens

With the support of the local community and the BCMA, a building was purchased in July 1999 for use as an Islamic Centre. The building had originally been built as a Chinese church in Nanaimo's historic Chinatown and was later used as a Francophone school by L'Association des Francophones de Nanaimo. It was officially opened as the Islamic Centre of Nanaimo on July 15, 2000, in the presence of city officials, community members, and local media.

2017

Expansion for a Growing Community

As the Muslim population in Nanaimo and surrounding areas grew, the original centre became too small for Friday and Eid prayers. Through the generous support of the community, a larger building was purchased in 2017 and prepared to serve the growing needs of the region.

Today

The Centre Today

The Nanaimo Chapter of the BCMA, operating from 897 Harbourview Street, continues to provide spiritual guidance, educational programs, and community services to Muslims throughout Mid-Vancouver Island.

Facility

Large prayer hall for men on the ground floor, a separate prayer hall for women, separate washrooms, and a basement with a kitchen and storage space.

Parking

The property includes parking space for approximately 40 vehicles, helping accommodate daily use as well as Friday and Eid attendance.

Regional Reach

The centre serves the wider Mid-Vancouver Island region and continues to welcome local families, students, and visitors.
